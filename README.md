## Overview

This repository contains the position paper introducing the unified perceptual substrate: an architecture for embodied predictive intelligence in which multiple sensory modalities, physics-derived predictions, bounded plastic adaptation, and active inference operate within a single representational medium.

The paper documents three years of independent research and establishes the architectural foundation for ReinforceAI's work on self-improving generalized intelligence inspired by the human brain.

## Architectural Commitments

The substrate is built on four load-bearing commitments:

**One unified medium.** Vision, audio, proprioception, and other modalities are configurations within one representational structure rather than separately processed streams.

**Prediction from physics, not reconstruction.** The substrate generates predictions through encoded physical principles. Sensors enter as corrective signals to predictions already in flight. Computation scales with prediction error, not sensor bandwidth.

**Markov blanket interface.** Structural separation between the internal substrate and the external environment. Information flows through designated channels only.

**Bounded plastic adaptation.** The encoded physics priors do not change. A separate plastic layer accumulates experience from coherent surprise above a threshold, preserving the integrity of the foundational priors.

The architecture converges with Karl Friston's free energy principle, derived here from first principles applied to the requirements of embodied intelligence.

## Convergence and Differentiation

The architecture aligns with established work in active inference, predictive coding, and the free energy principle. It differs from conventional embodied AI in that physics is encoded rather than learned, the perceptual substrate is unified across modalities at the representational level, and self-improvement is bounded by structural constraints rather than dependent on hoped-for alignment.
